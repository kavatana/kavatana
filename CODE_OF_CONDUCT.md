# Code of Conduct

This portfolio should be a useful and safe place for students, engineers,
reviewers, and teams learning to work with coding agents. Participation requires
respect for people, evidence, privacy, and honest authorship.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific when giving feedback.
- Critique ideas, code, and evidence without attacking the person.
- Welcome questions from people with different experience levels.
- Credit material human contributions accurately and with consent.
- Disclose AI assistance when the contribution workflow requests it.
- Keep credentials, personal data, private source, vulnerability details, and
  unpublished product context out of public threads.
- Correct mistakes openly and leave evidence that another person can inspect.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, personal attacks, or unwanted sexual
  attention.
- Doxxing, stalking, or publishing another person's private information.
- Spam, deceptive promotion, or staged issues, reviews, discussions, co-authors,
  or merges intended to manipulate activity or achievements.
- Knowingly false claims about tests, security, deployments, users, results, or
  authorship.
- Publishing exploit details or sensitive material after being directed to a
  private reporting route.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, releases, and
other spaces where someone officially represents this portfolio.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to `kavatanaa@gmail.com`. Include links,
dates, and only the evidence needed to understand the concern. Do not open a
public issue when a report contains personal or sensitive information.

The maintainer may remove content, request a correction, issue a warning, limit
participation, or permanently restrict access based on the seriousness and
pattern of behavior. Reports will be handled as privately as reasonably
possible. Retaliation against a reporter is not accepted.

The maintainer is responsible for applying this policy fairly and for seeking
an independent reviewer when directly involved in a report.
